# Brand directions — pick one

You said design from scratch. Here are three deliberately distinct directions. They're not variations of one idea — each makes a different bet about who Eik & Co is and who it's for. Pick one (or mix them, or reject all and ask for more) and I'll implement it across `theme.json`, the patterns, and a draft logo.

For each direction I include: **the bet**, **mood**, **palette**, **typography**, **logo treatment**, **voice samples**, **what kind of buyer it attracts**, and **what the homepage hero would feel like**.

---

## Direction A — "Quiet Confidence"

> Editorial, restrained, expensive-feeling. Looks like it could be the about page of an architect or a private wealth manager.

**The bet**: Norwegians buying a second home in Spain are often successful professionals in their 50s–60s. They've seen the cheap, brash, "VILLAS! POOLS! SUNSHINE!" Costa Blanca marketing for years and are quietly skeptical. A site that looks calm, considered, and unflashy reads as *competent* in this market.

**Mood**: Editorial magazine. Lots of whitespace. Generous typography. Photographs do the heavy lifting — buildings, light, landscape, hands signing papers. No stock-photo gloss.

**Palette**:
- **Base** `#f7f5f0` — warm off-white (paper)
- **Ink** `#1a1a1a` — near-black for text
- **Sand** `#d9cfc0` — section dividers
- **Accent** `#7a3d2a` — burnt sienna / Spanish terracotta (used sparingly — links, small flourishes)
- No green. No blue. Spain isn't a green-and-blue country, it's earth and stone.

**Typography**:
- **Display**: GT Sectra, Söhne, or Tiempos (paid) — alternative free: **Fraunces** (weight 300–500). Old-style serif with personality.
- **Body**: **Inter** or **IBM Plex Sans** at 18px / line-height 1.7. Generous, easy on the eye.
- **Monospace** (for footnotes, prices, dates): **JetBrains Mono** or system mono.

**Logo treatment**: A new wordmark in Fraunces, lowercase: `eik & co`. Optional roundel: a small circle containing "E&Co" for favicon and Instagram. No icon.

**Voice samples**:
- Hero: *"A buyer's agent for Ciudad Quesada. Independent, local, paid by the seller — never by you."*
- Section heading: *"How I work"*
- Button: *"Request a viewing"* (not "Get started" or "Click here")
- Tagline: *"Everyone is focused on making the best possible sale. You should be just as focused on making an equally good purchase."*

**Buyer it attracts**: 50–70 year-old Norwegian professionals, couples, considered decision-makers. Less impulse, more research.

**Hero feel**: Almost no copy. A single full-bleed photograph of a Costa Blanca rooftop or olive tree, edge of horizon. Headline in Fraunces, 80px, weight 400, three lines. One small button. That's the entire fold.

## Direction B — "Sun & Salt"

> Warm, modern Mediterranean. Looks like a contemporary boutique hotel or a wine import business. Optimistic, but grown-up.

**The bet**: Buyers are emotionally driven by *the dream of Spain* — sunshine, white walls, blue water, lemons. Lean into that. Make the site feel like the holiday they want, not like a legal document. The trust and seriousness comes through in the copy and the testimonial, not in the design language.

**Mood**: Warm, sun-bleached, summery without being a travel agent. Big bright photography, generous use of color, tactile feeling — terracotta tiles, white plaster, blue tile mosaics.

**Palette**:
- **Base** `#fffaf2` — warm cream
- **Ink** `#262221` — deep brown-black (warmer than pure black)
- **Sun** `#e8a23a` — saffron yellow (accent only, for buttons, highlights)
- **Sea** `#2b6e8c` — Mediterranean blue (links, small details)
- **Wash** `#f0e6d6` — light sand (section backgrounds)

**Typography**:
- **Display**: **DM Serif Display** or **Cormorant** — high-contrast serif, romantic but clean
- **Body**: **DM Sans** or **Manrope** — geometric, modern, friendly. 17px / 1.65
- Color: dark brown for body text, not black

**Logo treatment**: Wordmark `Eik & Co` in DM Serif Display. Optional small icon: an oak leaf (eik = oak in Norwegian — a literal nod to the name) in saffron, used as a bullet/separator and as the favicon.

**Voice samples**:
- Hero: *"Find your home in the Spanish sun — with someone who actually lives here."*
- Section heading: *"Where I work — and why"*
- Button: *"Plan a viewing trip"*
- Tagline: *"You wouldn't sell a house alone. Why buy one alone?"*

**Buyer it attracts**: Couples mid-40s through 60s, dreaming actively about retirement or a second home, ready to be charmed.

**Hero feel**: Big photo of white houses with bright blue shutters, palm trees in the foreground, oversaturated golden-hour light. Serif headline in DM Serif Display, 72px. Saffron yellow button. The page feels like sunshine.

## Direction C — "The Independent"

> Editorial-meets-newsletter. Almost defiantly minimal. Looks like a Substack or Stripe Press book site. The "anti-real-estate-website."

**The bet**: The entire Costa Blanca real estate industry — Norwegian and Spanish alike — looks identical: hero photos, agent in suit, contact form, identical templates. The most powerful move is to look *nothing like the rest*. Aim for the buyer who recognizes the difference between a real expert and someone with a Squarespace template.

**Mood**: Stripped-back, content-first, type-driven. Few photos, mostly text. Long-form, opinionated essays as the actual content — not "blog posts." The entire site reads like a respected industry insider writing publicly.

**Palette**:
- **Base** `#ffffff` — pure white
- **Ink** `#0a0a0a` — pure near-black
- **Subtle** `#5a5a5a` — muted gray for metadata, captions
- **Hairline** `#e8e8e8` — borders, dividers
- **Accent** `#c2410c` — single rust/orange for links and one CTA color
- That's it. Five colors total. No gradients. No images in the brand system itself.

**Typography**:
- **Display**: **Newsreader** (Google Fonts) — designed for reading at scale. Or **Source Serif 4**.
- **Body**: **Newsreader** (yes, same family — serif body text. Brave move, very readable, very editorial)
- **UI / nav / buttons**: **IBM Plex Mono** at small sizes — gives the site a "documentation site" feel
- All sizes calibrated for *reading*, not for showing off

**Logo treatment**: Plain text: `Eik & Co.` in Newsreader, weight 500. No icon. The period at the end is the entire logo treatment.

**Voice samples**:
- Hero: *"I'm an independent buyer's agent in Ciudad Quesada. Here's what I do, how I get paid, and why it matters."*
- Section heading: *"Notes on the Costa Blanca market — January"*
- Button: *"Read more →"* / *"Send a message →"*
- Tagline: *"Buyers don't have anyone in their corner. I'm in your corner."*

**Buyer it attracts**: Highly informed buyers, often second-home buyers who've done a transaction before and got burned by the standard process. They will read.

**Hero feel**: No photo. White background, single column of text. One huge headline in serif, two paragraphs of body text below it, one link. Looks like the front of a longread. Photos appear deeper in the page, used as evidence, not decoration.

## How to choose

Don't think about which is "best." Think about which one *Eik & Co actually is*, and which one makes you say "yes, that's the right tone."

| If you... | Pick |
| --- | --- |
| Want to look like you charge €10,000 for advice | **A — Quiet Confidence** |
| Want to look approachable and emotionally appealing | **B — Sun & Salt** |
| Want to be the only site in this market that doesn't look like a real-estate site | **C — The Independent** |

You can also tell me what you'd combine — e.g. "B's photography mood with A's typography and C's voice" — that's a legitimate answer too.

Once you pick, I'll:
1. Update `theme.json` with the chosen palette and type
2. Rewrite the patterns (`hero`, `viewing-cta`, `areas`) to match the chosen mood
3. Draft a logo (SVG) you can use as a starting point
4. Update the patterns' placeholder copy in the chosen voice
