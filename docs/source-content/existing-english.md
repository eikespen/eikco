## What is a buyer's agent? (process)

> A real estate agent can be your advisor and advocate throughout the complex process of buying property. Unlike traditional brokers who represent sellers, purchase brokers work only for you, the buyer, and ensure that your interests are taken care of at all stages.
>
> **The process:**
>
> 1. **Define your needs** — You start by defining your ideal property, including your desired location, type, budget and key features.
> 2. **The broker finds relevant properties** — The buyer's broker uses his market knowledge and network to find properties that meet your specific criteria.
> 3. **Efficient viewing and assessment** — The broker presents you with relevant options and arranges viewings on your schedule. They also give you professional advice and insight along the way.
> 4. **Thorough due diligence** — The buyer's agent carries out a thorough investigation of the property to uncover potential problems. This includes legal conditions, technical condition and financial aspects.
> 5. **Negotiation for you** — The broker negotiates prices and conditions on your behalf to ensure the best possible agreement.
> 6. **Smooth closing** — After you've made a decision, the buyer's agent handles the entire closing process, including inspections, appraisals, liaising with lawyers and financiers, and ensures a smooth handover.
>
> **The advantages of using a buying agent:**
>
> - **Informed decisions**: You get access to expertise and objective advice based on the market.
> - **Efficient search process**: You save time and unnecessary viewings by focusing on relevant properties.
> - **Stronger negotiating position**: The broker negotiates on your behalf and works to secure the best possible price.
> - **Safer process**: You get help with due diligence and guidance throughout the entire purchase process.
>
> Investing in a real estate agent can be a smart investment that gives you peace of mind, informed choices and a smoother real estate transaction.

**Editor's note**: Strong structure. Use as the basis for `/how-it-works`. Tighten language: "real estate agent" → "buyer's agent" throughout for consistency.

## How does the buyer's agent get paid?

> Although buyer's agents often receive a fee from the seller's agent through a pre-negotiated sharing arrangement, it is important to understand the nuances of this agreement:
>
> - **Transparency is key**: Discuss the commission structure and distribution openly with your buying broker in advance.
> - **No hidden costs**: Even if you, the buyer, do not directly pay the broker's fee, the commission is still included in the final sale price of the property.
> - **Mutual benefit**: The seller pays the commission to get professional representation from both brokers, which ensures a smooth and efficient transaction for all parties involved.
> - **Negotiability**: Commission percentage and distribution are negotiable, so discuss your preferences with both brokers to reach a mutual agreement.
>
> Remember that your buyer's agent must be your trusted adviser and look after your interests throughout the process. Choose a broker who prioritizes clear communication and ethical practices to ensure a positive and informed buying experience.

**Editor's note**: Excellent material for a FAQ entry or a dedicated "How I get paid" section. The transparency angle is strong.

## How I work

> **Mapping.** Everyone has different preferences, such as the size of the property, price, location, type of property (detached house, apartment, or townhouse), etc. To help me find the best properties for you easily, please fill out a Property Form that gives me an idea of what you want.
>
> **I am searching in the market.** With your preferences in mind, I do a careful job of surveying any homes that may be relevant for you. As soon as I have obtained enough information about the homes I have found and have carried out the necessary legal checks on the homes, I will send them over to you by e-mail. If the homes are of interest, I contact the seller and arrange a viewing of the home.
>
> **Viewings.** If you yourself have the opportunity to come to Spain for the screenings, I will book these in for you so that you get the most efficient possible trip down to Spain if screenings are on the agenda.
