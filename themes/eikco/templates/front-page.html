On buying

Everyone is focused on making the best possible sale. You should be just as focused on making an equally good purchase.

In Spain almost every real-estate agent represents the seller. They set the asking price, run the viewings, and close the deal — all on the seller's side of the table. As a buyer you're expected to do all that in a language you don't speak, in a legal system you don't know, from a country away. Most people just go along with whatever agent they happened to walk into first, and hope for the best.

I'm the person on your side of the table. I find properties worth your time, I do the legal and structural checks before you ever see them, I come with you to viewings, I negotiate the price, and I stay with the process all the way to the notary. The seller pays the commission, not you. There is no retainer, no monthly fee, and no bill at the end.
